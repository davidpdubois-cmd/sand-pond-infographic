The Heart of the Case

Nineteen of twenty-one lots carry some form of anti-commercial restriction written by the same grantor over the same period — a pattern Maine courts recognize as a common scheme of development. Under this doctrine, the restrictions bind every parcel in the scheme, including the land Miriam Siddall retained. The developer’s parcel is not exempt.
